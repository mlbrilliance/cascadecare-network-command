# Business Associate Agreement — Provider Alpha

**Covered Entity:** Provider Alpha (an urban academic medical center)
**Business Associate:** ClearFlow Health Network ("ClearFlow")
**Effective Date:** March 15, 2025
**Services:** Claims pricing and payment routing for Provider Alpha and its affiliated faculty practice plan

This Agreement governs ClearFlow's Use and Disclosure of PHI under HIPAA, the HITECH
Act, and 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164.

## 1. Definitions

As set forth in 45 CFR §§ 160.103 and 164.501. This Agreement additionally covers
**research-related PHI** disclosed to ClearFlow solely for claims adjudication; ClearFlow
shall not Use such PHI for research or de-identification without separate authorization.

## 2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

2.1 ClearFlow may Use or Disclose PHI as necessary to perform the Services, for
ClearFlow's proper management and administration, and as Required by Law.

2.2 ClearFlow shall apply the minimum necessary standard (45 CFR § 164.502(b)).

## 3. Safeguards

ClearFlow shall maintain Security Rule safeguards (45 CFR §§ 164.308–312) and shall make
its policies available to Provider Alpha on reasonable request for audit.

## 4. Breach Notification

ClearFlow shall notify Provider Alpha of a Breach of Unsecured PHI **without unreasonable
delay and no later than fifteen (15) calendar days** after Discovery.

## 5. Disclosures Required by Law; Legal Process

5.1 ClearFlow **may disclose PHI when Required by Law**, including in response to a
subpoena, court order, or administrative request that meets 45 CFR § 164.512(e) or (f).

5.2 ClearFlow shall provide Provider Alpha notice of any such disclosure **promptly after
disclosure**; no pre-disclosure notice or waiting period is required.

## 6. Subcontractors

ClearFlow may engage subcontractors (including the Nimbus Patient Engagement Platform)
provided ClearFlow gives Provider Alpha **written notice** and binds the subcontractor by
flow-down agreement (45 CFR § 164.502(e)(1)(ii)). Prior approval is not required.

## 7. Term and Termination

Terminates on return/destruction of PHI or material breach uncured within thirty (30)
days. Sections 4 and 5 survive.

## 8. Indemnification

Mutual indemnification for each party's own HIPAA violations.
