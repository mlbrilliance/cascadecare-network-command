# Business Associate Agreement — Provider Delta

**Covered Entity:** Provider Delta (a specialty surgical center)
**Business Associate:** ClearFlow Health Network ("ClearFlow")
**Effective Date:** February 1, 2025
**Services:** Claims pricing and payment routing for surgical episodes

This Agreement governs ClearFlow's Use and Disclosure of PHI under HIPAA, the HITECH
Act, and 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164. The PHI scope is limited to surgical claim and
episode data; ClearFlow receives no broader medical record.

## 1. Definitions

As set forth in 45 CFR §§ 160.103 and 164.501. "Covered PHI" is limited to the data
elements enumerated in Exhibit A (procedure, pricing, and payment fields).

## 2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

2.1 ClearFlow may Use or Disclose Covered PHI only as necessary to perform the Services.

2.2 ClearFlow shall strictly apply the minimum necessary standard and shall not request,
Use, or Disclose PHI outside the Covered PHI scope.

## 3. Safeguards

ClearFlow shall maintain Security Rule safeguards (45 CFR §§ 164.308–312).

## 4. Breach Notification

ClearFlow shall notify Provider Delta of a Breach of Unsecured PHI **no later than ten
(10) business days** after Discovery.

## 5. Disclosures Required by Law; Legal Process

5.1 Upon receipt of a subpoena or compulsory legal process, ClearFlow **may comply** after
providing Provider Delta **written notice at least seven (7) calendar days before
disclosure**.

5.2 ClearFlow shall disclose only the minimum necessary Covered PHI responsive to the
legal process and shall object to any demand seeking PHI outside the Covered PHI scope.

## 6. Subcontractors

ClearFlow may engage subcontractors (including the Nimbus Patient Engagement Platform)
under flow-down terms (45 CFR § 164.502(e)(1)(ii)) limited to the Covered PHI scope.

## 7. Term and Termination

Terminates on return/destruction of PHI or material breach uncured within thirty (30)
days. Sections 4 and 5 survive.

## 8. Indemnification

Mutual indemnification for each party's own HIPAA violations.
